We discuss the relationship between the proposed framework and prior work, note
the significance of the approach if successful, and identify questions for future
investigation.

\subsection{Relationship to the Hilbert--Pólya Conjecture}

The Hilbert--Pólya conjecture proposes that the imaginary parts of the nontrivial
zeros of $\zeta$ are eigenvalues of a self-adjoint operator. A self-adjoint
operator on a Hilbert space has a real spectrum, so the conjecture, if realized,
would immediately imply that zeros lie on the critical line (since $s = \tfrac{1}{2} + it$
with $t$ real corresponds to $\Re(s) = \tfrac{1}{2}$).

The present approach does not directly construct a Hilbert--Pólya operator.
Instead, it proposes a balance interpretation in which zeros correspond to
cancellation states. If the spectral coherence operator $H$ of \S4 can be
shown to be self-adjoint, the two approaches may converge.

\subsection{Relationship to Connes's Approach}

Connes~\cite{Connes1999} constructs a spectral realization of the zeros of $\zeta$
using a noncommutative space built from the adele ring and the action of the idele
class group. His approach produces a trace formula analogous to Selberg's, in which
the zeros appear as spectral data.

The present approach is more elementary in its foundational machinery, relying on
the functional equation directly rather than on adelic harmonic analysis. Whether
the present framework can be embedded into or derived from Connes's construction
is an open question.

\subsection{Relationship to Random Matrix Theory}

The Montgomery--Odlyzko law~\cite{Montgomery1973, Odlyzko1987} describes the
pair correlation of zeros of $\zeta$ as matching the GUE (Gaussian Unitary Ensemble)
statistics of random Hermitian matrices. This provides strong evidence for the
existence of a Hilbert--Pólya operator and for RH, but does not constitute a proof.

The proposed spectral-coherence framework does not directly predict or use the
GUE statistics. It would be interesting to determine whether the balance criterion
has implications for the distribution of zeros along the critical line.

\subsection{Potential Significance}

If the proposed framework can be made rigorous — specifically, if the spectral
operator $H$ can be fully defined and the correspondence and uniqueness conjectures
proved — the result would constitute a proof of the Riemann Hypothesis. Such a
proof would have implications for:
\begin{itemize}
  \item The distribution of prime numbers (via the explicit formula relating zeros
        of $\zeta$ to primes).
  \item Numerous conjectures in analytic number theory that are known to follow
        from RH.
  \item The theoretical foundations of random matrix theory as applied to number theory.
\end{itemize}

These potential implications are noted here for context, not as motivation for
overstating the current state of the work.

\subsection{Open Questions for Future Investigation}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Can $H$ be constructed as a self-adjoint operator, and if so, does this
        give a proof of the Hilbert--Pólya conjecture as a corollary?
  \item Is the balance criterion equivalent to any known analytic condition on
        $\zeta$?
  \item Does the framework generalize to $L$-functions, where the analogs of RH
        are also open?
  \item Are there computable consequences of the balance criterion that could be
        tested numerically?
\end{enumerate}
