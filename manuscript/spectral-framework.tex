This section develops the proposed spectral-coherence structure. The construction
is currently at the stage of a detailed proposal; items requiring further rigorous
development are marked explicitly.

\subsection{Motivation}

The Hilbert--Pólya conjecture proposes that there exists a self-adjoint operator
whose eigenvalues are the imaginary parts of the nontrivial zeros of $\zeta$.
If such an operator $\mathcal{A}$ exists, the reality of its spectrum would imply
that zeros lie on the critical line.

The present framework takes a related but distinct approach. Rather than seeking
an operator whose eigenvalues reproduce the zero locations, the manuscript proposes
an operator $H$ whose \emph{balance points} — states of spectral cancellation —
correspond to zeros of $\zeta$. The balance interpretation is intended to make
the role of reflection symmetry more explicit.

\subsection{Spectral Coherence and Balance}

The term \emph{spectral coherence} refers informally to the property of $H$ at
a point $s$ that makes $\Phi(s) = 0$ possible. The physical intuition is that
at a zero of $\zeta$, the spectral contributions from $s$ and its reflection
$\sigma(s) = 1-s$ cancel perfectly — destructive interference in the spectral
structure.

This cancellation can only occur, by the proposed argument, when $s = \sigma(s)$,
i.e., when $\Re(s) = \tfrac{1}{2}$. The argument for this is developed in
\S7 and \S8 (Proof Architecture and Critical Line Argument).

\begin{remark}
  The intuition of ``destructive interference'' is an analogy, not a theorem.
  The argument of \S8 must establish, without reference to analogy, that the
  relevant cancellation is impossible for $\Re(s) \ne \tfrac{1}{2}$.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Relationship to Prior Spectral Constructions}

Several prior constructions propose operators related to $\zeta$:
\begin{itemize}
  \item The Berry--Keating operator $H = xp - \tfrac{1}{4}$ (on $L^2(\R_+)$)
        has a formal trace formula related to $\zeta$, but its self-adjointness
        is not established in the standard sense.
  \item Connes's construction uses an adelic space and an action of the idele
        class group to obtain a spectral interpretation of the zeros.
  \item Sierra--Townsend's Landau level model gives a physical Hamiltonian whose
        eigenvalues approximate the zero spacings.
\end{itemize}
