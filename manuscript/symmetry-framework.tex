This section isolates what can be obtained from functional symmetry alone.

Let $\sigma(s)=1-s$. The critical line is the fixed-point locus of $\sigma$. The equation $\xi(s)=\xi(1-s)$ implies that the nontrivial zero set is symmetric under reflection about that line.

\begin{remark}
Symmetry alone does not exclude off-line zeros; it only enforces paired structure. Any RH argument must add a separate exclusion mechanism.
\end{remark}
