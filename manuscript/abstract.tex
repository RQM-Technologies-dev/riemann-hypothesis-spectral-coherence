This manuscript presents a conservative drafting structure for a proposed proof framework related to the Riemann Hypothesis (RH). The organizing theme is a spectral-coherence interpretation of the reflection symmetry of the completed zeta function. The present document does \emph{not} claim a proof of RH. Instead, it records a proof program, states draft theorem targets, and marks unresolved bridges explicitly for mathematical audit.

The core drafting goals are: (i) fix notation and assumptions, (ii) isolate the exact claims that would imply RH under stated hypotheses, and (iii) separate heuristic intuition from formal obligations. Sections on proof architecture and limitations are treated as primary control points for rigor.
