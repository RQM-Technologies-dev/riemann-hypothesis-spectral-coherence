We record the logical dependency chain as a proof program for audit.  This section specifies required lemmas and bridges, but does not claim that those bridges are proved.

\subsection*{Program-level dependency graph}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Formal setup and admissible objects
    \item Operator construction and analytic control
    \item Balance functional well-posedness
    \item Zero--balance correspondence (Target A)
    \item Critical-line uniqueness of balanced points (Target B)
    \item Global off-line exclusion mechanism
    \item Final RH reduction inside the framework
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Stage 1: Formal setup and admissible class}
\textbf{Required inputs.}
Precise definitions of the state space $H$, admissible data class, and the balance criterion; explicit regularity, decay, and symmetry assumptions.

\textbf{Intended output.}
A fixed formal context in which all subsequent operators and functionals are well-typed (domain/codomain, topology, and admissibility constraints).

\textbf{Current status.}
\textbf{Established} as framework definitions (subject to local clarification edits as notation evolves).

\subsection*{Stage 2: Operator construction and analytic control}
\textbf{Required inputs.}
Stage 1 definitions, plus a concrete operator construction rule and hypotheses needed for boundedness/closability/spectral meaning (as appropriate to the chosen operator model).

\textbf{Intended output.}
A mathematically controlled operator layer: existence of the constructed operator(s), admissible action on $H$, and a traceable list of analytic properties actually used later.

\textbf{Current status.}
\textbf{Partial}.  Core construction ideas are present, but several control statements remain assumption-level and require formal proofs under explicit hypotheses.

\subsection*{Stage 3: Balance functional well-posedness}
\textbf{Required inputs.}
Stages 1--2, including regularity and operator-control bounds sufficient to define the balance functional on the intended class.

\textbf{Intended output.}
Well-posedness package: definition on a specified domain, finiteness, continuity/differentiability properties needed downstream, and invariance/covariance statements used in correspondence arguments.

\textbf{Current status.}
\textbf{Partial}.  The functional is specified at the formal level; full well-posedness proofs are not complete in all parameter regimes.

\subsection*{Stage 4: Zero--balance correspondence bridge (Target A)}
\textbf{Required inputs.}
Stage 3 well-posedness plus a precise matching rule between balanced configurations and zeros of $\zeta$ in the manuscript's encoding.

\textbf{Intended output.}
A bidirectional correspondence theorem (or clearly one-directional theorem plus separate converse):
\[
\text{balanced point} \Longleftrightarrow \text{encoded zeta zero},
\]
with all side conditions explicit.

\textbf{Current status.}
\textbf{Open bridge}.  Target A (\cref{thm:target-correspondence}) is not yet proved.
\openbridge{Requires a rigorous, hypothesis-complete map between the balance condition and the zero set of $\zeta$, including injectivity/surjectivity conditions and exclusion of spurious balanced states.}

\subsection*{Stage 5: Critical-line uniqueness bridge (Target B)}
\textbf{Required inputs.}
Stage 4 correspondence plus structural constraints sufficient to force balanced configurations onto $\Re(s)=\tfrac12$.

\textbf{Intended output.}
A theorem-level \emph{localization} statement, with explicit regime and assumptions:
\[
\Phi_H(s)=0 \Longrightarrow \Re(s)=\tfrac12
\]
for all admissible $s$ in the Stage-5 regime.
This stage is allowed to be local/regime-scoped, provided the scope is explicit.

\textbf{Current status.}
\textbf{Open bridge}.  Target B (\cref{thm:target-uniqueness}) remains unproved.
\openbridge{Requires a fully rigorous uniqueness-of-balance theorem target with explicit hypotheses, explicit regime of validity, and explicit non-spuriousness conditions for the balance predicate.}

\subsection*{Stage 6: Global off-line exclusion mechanism}
\textbf{Required inputs.}
Refined estimates or structural principles compatible with Stages 2--5, sufficient to upgrade local uniqueness logic to a global exclusion statement.
The manuscript now treats this as a \emph{candidate exclusion mechanism template} problem: the Stage-6 proof must identify at least one explicit theorem mechanism class (for example coercive gap, positivity/monotonicity, rigidity equality case, reflection-incompatibility, or another formally stated alternative) and show that it applies on the full admissible strip.

\textbf{Intended output.}
A global theorem-level exclusion mechanism distinct from Stage 5:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item transfer of Stage-5 localization hypotheses from local regime to full admissible strip,
  \item full-strip exclusion of admissible off-line balanced configurations,
  \item symmetry-compatible transfer (reflection/conjugation) without weakening hypotheses.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Current status.}
\textbf{Open}.  A mechanism template is now explicit, but no model-specific exclusion theorem is proved.
\openbridge{Requires proof-quality global control, including boundary-near and large-parameter regimes; requires an explicit contradiction theorem from admissible off-line balance under one synchronized hypothesis package; computational checks may guide conjecture but do not substitute for the exclusion proof.}

\subsection*{Stage 7: Final RH reduction inside the framework}
\textbf{Required inputs.}
Completed Stages 4--6 with hypotheses synchronized and no circular dependencies.

\textbf{Intended output.}
A clean conditional reduction:
\begin{quote}
If Target A and Target B (with the global exclusion package) hold under assumptions $\mathcal{A}$, then RH follows within this framework.
\end{quote}

\textbf{Current status.}
\textbf{Open/conditional}.  Reduction logic is straightforward once bridges are proved, but present manuscript status is conditional on unresolved bridges.

\begin{remark}
Audit interpretation: failure of any open bridge (Stages 4--6) blocks the RH reduction.  This section is a dependency map for review, not a claim that those bridges are complete.
\end{remark}
