The proof of the Riemann Hypothesis, if achievable via the present framework, would
proceed through the following five steps. The logical dependencies between steps
are described after the outline. Steps that are not currently proved are marked
explicitly.

\subsection*{Step 1: Establish the Symmetry Framework}

\textbf{Goal:} Show that the functional equation $\xi(s) = \xi(1-s)$ implies that
the critical line $\Re(s) = \tfrac{1}{2}$ is the unique axis of a well-defined
reflection symmetry in the critical strip.

\textbf{Status:} \emph{Complete from the literature.} See
Proposition~\ref{prop:functional-equation} and the development in \S3.

\textbf{Key output:} The reflection $\sigma \colon s \mapsto 1-s$ is an involution
with fixed-point locus $\Re(s) = \tfrac{1}{2}$. The zero set of $\zeta$ is closed
under $\sigma$.

\subsection*{Step 2: Define the Spectral/Coherence Object}

\textbf{Goal:} Construct the spectral coherence operator $H$ (Definition~\ref{def:H})
as a mathematically well-defined linear operator on a specified Hilbert or Banach space,
symmetric under $\sigma$.

\textbf{Status:} \gap{In progress. The operator $H$ is not yet fully defined.
This step is a prerequisite for all subsequent steps and must be completed before
the argument can proceed.}

\textbf{Key output:} A precise mathematical object $H$ with a well-defined domain,
codomain, spectrum, and symmetry property $H \sim H \circ \sigma$ (unitary equivalence).

\subsection*{Step 3: Correspondence Between Zeros and Balance Points}

\textbf{Goal:} Prove Conjecture~\ref{conj:correspondence}: the nontrivial zeros of
$\zeta$ in the critical strip are exactly the balance points of $H$.

\textbf{Status:} \gap{Open. This requires both Step~2 to be complete and a rigorous
calculation or argument relating the spectral properties of $H$ to the zeros of $\zeta$.
This is the most analytically demanding step.}

\textbf{Key output:} A theorem of the form:
\[
  \zeta(s) = 0 \iff s \text{ satisfies the balance criterion for } H.
\]

\subsection*{Step 4: Prove That Only the Critical Line Supports Balance}

\textbf{Goal:} Prove Conjecture~\ref{conj:uniqueness}: the balance criterion is
satisfiable only at points $s$ with $\Re(s) = \tfrac{1}{2}$.

\textbf{Status:} \gap{Open. This is the central open gap in the argument. The
proposed mechanism is that the reflection symmetry of $H$ combined with the
structure of the balance criterion forces balance states to the fixed-point locus
$\Re(s) = \tfrac{1}{2}$. This must be proved, not merely argued by analogy.}

\textbf{Key output:} A theorem of the form:
\[
  s \text{ satisfies the balance criterion} \implies \Re(s) = \tfrac{1}{2}.
\]

\subsection*{Step 5: Derive the Impossibility of Off-Line Zeros}

\textbf{Goal:} Combine Steps~3 and~4 to conclude that there are no nontrivial
zeros of $\zeta$ off the critical line, completing the proof of RH.

\textbf{Status:} \emph{Contingent on Steps~2–4.} If Steps~3 and~4 are proved,
Step~5 is a direct logical consequence:
\begin{align*}
  \zeta(s) = 0
  &\implies s \text{ satisfies the balance criterion}
    &&\text{(Step 3)} \\
  &\implies \Re(s) = \tfrac{1}{2}.
    &&\text{(Step 4)}
\end{align*}

\subsection*{Logical Dependencies}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ccccc}
  Step 1 & $\to$ & Step 2 & $\to$ & Step 3 \\
         &       & Step 2 & $\to$ & Step 4 \\
         &       & Step 3, Step 4 & $\to$ & Step 5
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Steps~1 is established. Step~2 is the foundational construction task. Steps~3
and~4 are the core proof obligations. Step~5 is the logical conclusion.

The critical bottleneck is Step~4. Even if Step~3 is established, the framework
does not prove RH unless Step~4 — uniqueness of the balance axis — is also proved.
