This section develops the argument for the privilege of the critical line
$\Re(s) = \tfrac{1}{2}$ within the proposed spectral-coherence framework.
The argument proceeds from the structure established in \S3 (symmetry) and \S4
(spectral framework).

\subsection{Statement of the Goal}

We aim to establish Conjecture~\ref{conj:uniqueness}: \emph{the balance criterion
is satisfiable only on $\Re(s) = \tfrac{1}{2}$}. Equivalently (assuming
Conjecture~\ref{conj:correspondence}): \emph{all nontrivial zeros of $\zeta$
lie on the critical line.}

The argument is organized as follows:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(A)] Show that the balance criterion is compatible with reflection symmetry
             in a way that forces balance points to the fixed-point locus.
  \item[(B)] Show that the balance criterion is \emph{not} compatible with a
             pair $(s_0, 1-s_0)$ with $\Re(s_0) \ne \tfrac{1}{2}$.
\end{enumerate}

Step~(A) is Lemma~\ref{lem:balance-symmetric}; step~(B) is Lemma~\ref{lem:offlineasymmetry}.
Both lemmas are currently \gap{open}.

\subsection{The Symmetry Argument}

Assume (pending proof of Lemma~\ref{lem:balance-symmetric}) that the balance
criterion is symmetric: if $s$ satisfies it, so does $\sigma(s) = 1-s$.

For $s$ on the critical line: $\sigma(s) = s$, so the symmetry condition is
trivially consistent.

For $s$ off the critical line: $s \ne \sigma(s)$, and both $s$ and $1-s$ would
need to satisfy the balance criterion simultaneously. The proposed argument
claims this is impossible.

\gap{The impossibility of simultaneous balance at $s$ and $1-s$ for off-line $s$
is the statement of Lemma~\ref{lem:offlineasymmetry} and is the central open step.
No rigorous proof is currently available.}

\subsection{Proposed Contradiction Argument for Off-Line Zeros}

Suppose for contradiction that $s_0$ satisfies the balance criterion with
$\Re(s_0) = \sigma_0 \ne \tfrac{1}{2}$. By Lemma~\ref{lem:balance-symmetric}
(not yet proved), $\sigma(s_0) = 1 - s_0$ also satisfies the balance criterion.

Now $\Re(1 - s_0) = 1 - \sigma_0 \ne \tfrac{1}{2}$ (since $\sigma_0 \ne \tfrac{1}{2}$).
The pair $(s_0, 1-s_0)$ lies symmetrically about the critical line, at distances
$|\sigma_0 - \tfrac{1}{2}|$ on either side.

The proposed claim is that the spectral cancellation required for balance at both
$s_0$ and $1-s_0$ simultaneously is impossible:
\[
  \Phi(s_0) = 0 \text{ and } \Phi(1-s_0) = 0
  \implies \gap{\text{contradiction with the structure of } H}.
\]

\gap{The nature of this contradiction must be derived from the definition of $H$
and $\Phi$. It cannot be stated precisely until those definitions are complete.}

\subsection{What Must Be Proved}

The following items must be established for the critical-line argument to be complete:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{(Step 2 of proof architecture)} The operator $H$ and the
        coherence functional $\Phi$ must be fully defined.
  \item \textbf{(Lemma~\ref{lem:balance-symmetric})} The balance criterion must
        be proved symmetric under $\sigma$.
  \item \textbf{(Lemma~\ref{lem:offlineasymmetry})} Off-line balance must be
        proved impossible.
  \item \textbf{(Conjecture~\ref{conj:correspondence})} The correspondence between
        zeros of $\zeta$ and balance points of $H$ must be established.
\end{enumerate}

Until all four items are proved, the critical-line argument is incomplete.

\begin{remark}
  The argument as currently sketched is logically coherent but not rigorous.
  Each of the four items above requires nontrivial mathematical work. The
  framework is designed so that, if these items are established, the conclusion
  (RH) follows by clean logical deduction. But the framework itself is not a proof.
\end{remark}
