## Foundational References

**Riemann, B. (1859)**
"Ueber die Anzahl der Primzahlen unter einer gegebenen Grösse."
*Monatsberichte der Berliner Akademie.*
— The original paper introducing the zeta function and the Riemann Hypothesis.

**Davenport, H. (2000)**
*Multiplicative Number Theory*, 3rd ed. Springer.
— Standard reference for the analytic theory of the zeta function.

**Titchmarsh, E. C. (1986)**
*The Theory of the Riemann Zeta-Function*, 2nd ed. Oxford University Press.
— Comprehensive treatment of the zeta function, including the functional equation
and the distribution of zeros.

**Edwards, H. M. (1974)**
*Riemann's Zeta Function.* Academic Press.
— Detailed exposition of Riemann's original argument and subsequent developments.

## Spectral Approaches to RH

**Hilbert, D. / Pólya, G. (ca. 1910s–1950s)**
The Hilbert–Pólya conjecture (oral / informal): the imaginary parts of the
nontrivial zeros are eigenvalues of a self-adjoint operator.
— The motivating conjecture for operator-theoretic approaches to RH.

**Berry, M. V., & Keating, J. P. (1999)**
"H = xp and the Riemann zeros."
*Supersymmetry and Trace Formulae: Chaos and Disorder*, Springer.
— Proposes a specific non-self-adjoint Hamiltonian whose quantization is related
to the zeros of ζ.

**Connes, A. (1999)**
"Trace formula in noncommutative geometry and the zeros of the Riemann zeta function."
*Selecta Mathematica*, 5(1), 29–106.
— Constructs a spectral realization of the zeros using noncommutative geometry
and adelic methods.

**Sierra, G., & Townsend, P. K. (2008)**
"Landau levels and Riemann zeros."
*Physical Review Letters*, 101, 110201.
— A physical model linking zeros of ζ to Landau levels.

## Random Matrix Theory and Statistical Properties

**Montgomery, H. L. (1973)**
"The pair correlation of zeros of the zeta function."
*Analytic Number Theory, Proc. Symposia Pure Math.*, vol. 24, pp. 181–193.
— The origin of the GUE connection.

**Odlyzko, A. M. (1987)**
"On the distribution of spacings between zeros of the zeta function."
*Mathematics of Computation*, 48(177), 273–308.
— Extensive numerical evidence for GUE statistics.
